User Feedback: Regularly collect user feedback to understand their needs and challenges, using this insight to iterate on the app.

General Approach to Creating User Flows
Identify the User’s Goal: Start by understanding the primary objective the user wants to achieve with the app.

Entry Point: Determine how the user will enter the app (e.g., app icon, notification, or external link).

Major Steps: List the major actions the user needs to take to achieve their goal.

Decision Points: Identify any decisions the user must make that could alter their path.

Completion: Define what it looks like when the user successfully achieves their goal.

Exit or Next Steps: Decide where the user goes after completing their goal - do they exit, or is there a logical next step?

Example: Farmer (Primary User)
Goal: Update inventory after harvest.

Entry Point: Taps app icon on smartphone.

Major Steps: a. Log in (if not already logged in). b. Navigate to the "Inventory" section from the main menu. c. Select "Add New Harvest" to update inventory. d. Fill in details of the harvest (e.g., crop type, quantity). e. Submit the updated inventory.

Decision Points: a. Choose to sell some of the harvest directly through the app. b. Decide to use the app's recommendation for the best selling time based on market prices.

Completion: Receives confirmation that inventory has been updated.

Exit or Next Steps: a. If choosing to sell, navigate to the "Marketplace" section. b. If done, either log out or return to the main menu for other activities.

Designing an app structure involves outlining the main components and functionalities that the application will offer to its users. For the purpose of this exercise, let's outline the app structure for the hypothetical Fataplus app, which is aimed at supporting Malagasy farmers with features ranging from stock management to educational resources. The app structure below is designed to cater to the diverse needs of its users, including farmers, agricultural workers, agribusiness owners, and more.



Fataplus App Structure Overview:

Home Dashboard



Overview: Quick insights and notifications about the user's farming activities, weather alerts, market prices, and latest agricultural news.

Customizable Widgets: Users can personalize their dashboard with widgets like weather forecasts, task reminders, or market trends.



2. Profile Management



Personal Profile: For individual users to manage their account details, preferences, and settings.

Farm Profile: Where users can add information about their farm(s), including size, location, crop types, and farming practices.



3. Task Management



Daily Tasks: A list of daily farming tasks personalized based on the farm profile and season.

Task Assignments: For farm managers to assign tasks to workers, track progress, and receive updates.



4. Inventory & Stock Management



Seed Stock: Management of seed inventory, including varieties, quantities, and planting schedules.

Harvested Crops: Tracking of harvested stock, storage details, and readiness for sale.

Supplies Inventory: Management of farming supplies like fertilizers, pesticides, and equipment.



5. Market Insights & Trading



Market Prices: Real-time data on local and regional market prices for various crops.

Selling Platform: A platform for listing produce for sale, negotiating with buyers, and tracking transactions.

Purchase Supplies: For users to buy farming supplies directly through the app, with options for home delivery or local pickup.



6. Financial Management



Expense Tracking: Tool for recording farming expenses and categorizing them for better financial management.

Income Tracking: Monitor revenue from sold crops and other farm-generated income.

Budget Planning: Assistance in planning annual or seasonal budgets based on historical data and future projections.



7. Educational Resources



Articles & Guides: Library of articles, guides, and tutorials on best farming practices, new technologies, and sustainable agriculture.

Video Tutorials: Video content covering a wide range of topics from basic farming techniques to advanced agricultural technologies.

Community Forum: A space for farmers to ask questions, share experiences, and connect with agricultural experts.



8. Notifications & Alerts



Weather Alerts: Timely notifications about weather conditions affecting farming activities.

Task Reminders: Reminders about upcoming tasks, planting schedules, or other important deadlines.

Market Alerts: Notifications about significant changes in market prices or opportunities to sell produce at favorable rates.



9. Support & Feedback



Help Center: Access to FAQs, troubleshooting guides, and contact information for direct support.

Feedback Submission: Feature for users to submit feedback, report bugs, or suggest new features for the app.



Additional Considerations:



Multi-Language Support: Considering the diverse linguistic landscape of Madagascar, offering the app in multiple languages can increase accessibility.

Offline Functionality: Given the potential connectivity issues in rural areas, critical features like task management, inventory tracking, and educational resources should be accessible offline.



This app structure is designed to provide a comprehensive suite of tools for Malagasy farmers, helping them to manage their farming operations more efficiently, make informed decisions based on real-time data, and access valuable educational content to improve their practices and livelihoods.
